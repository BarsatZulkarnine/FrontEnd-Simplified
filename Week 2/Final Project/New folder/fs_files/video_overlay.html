The Basics
Heroes

Players choose a Hero at the start of every match. There are 121 Heroes in Dota 2, each with their own unique set of Abilities and playstyle.

There are three basic categories of Heroes: Strength, Agility, and Intelligence.

Heroes are further broken down into Ranged and Melee, which are defined by the range of their attacks. Melee Heroes must be close to their targets, while Ranged Heroes can attack from afar.

Heroes have three basic Abilities and one powerful Ultimate Ability that is unlocked at Level 6.

The Basics
Items

Heroes can equip Items that augment and improve their attributes and Abilities. In this way, Items can change how a Hero is played.

Items can be purchased with Gold from the Shopkeeper located in each team's Fountain.

Some Items can be consumed to replenish a Hero's Health and Mana.

Many powerful Items may only be purchased at two Secret Shops, which are located deeper within the Map.

Items you purchase can often be used as components in Recipes, which can then be combined into powerful upgraded Items. Upgraded Items can also be sold back to the Shopkeeper for half of their original purchase price.

The Basics
Lanes, Creeps & Towers

Each team's Base has three sets of Barracks that spawn waves of Units called Creeps that march across the Map to the other team's Base. The path these Creeps take are called Lanes.

Heroes earn Experience by killing Creeps, and Gold when they land the killing blow on Creeps.

Spaced at intervals in each Lane there are three powerful Towers that will automatically attack enemy Heroes and Creeps that get near them.

In addition to Creeps that march down the Lanes, there are Neutral Creep Camps, at various locations on the Map, which can also be killed for Gold, Experience, and Special Items.

The Battlefield
Minimap

The Minimap provides a condensed view of the entire Map, showing the position of visible, allied and enemy Heroes, Creeps, Structures and Wards.

The Battlefield
Lanes

Lanes describe the path along which allied Creeps march toward the enemy Ancient. Creeps start marching from the Barracks that spawn them, and there are three defensive Towers placed along the path leaving each Base.

Top, Bottom & Mid
Top Lane runs along the North and West edge of the Map. Mid Lane runs diagonally through the Map, with its midway point in the River. Bottom Lane runs along the South and East edges of the Map.
Safe & OffLane
The Radiant portion of the Bottom Lane and the Dire portion of the Top Lane are often referred to as the Safe, or Easy Lane. The Radiant portion of the Top Lane and the Dire portion of the Bottom Lane are often referred to as the Off, or Hard Lane.
